Prompt Engineering and the Functional Mindset August 2025

Functional programming has long appealed to me for the clarity it brings: small, composable functions, transparent data transformations, and a declarative style that emphasizes “what” over “how.” Lately, I’ve been struck by how similar instincts arise when working with large language models through prompt engineering.

A good prompt, like a well-crafted function, isolates assumptions, defines inputs clearly, and produces outputs that can be composed with others. Both are fragile when overloaded with implicit context, yet surprisingly robust when treated as small, testable building blocks. This analogy is imperfect, of course, but it has helped me think about prompts less as magic incantations and more as artifacts subject to the same discipline I value in programming.

I expect to return to this parallel in future notes, exploring whether functional abstractions might suggest better ways to design, test, or even generate prompts systematically. For now, I leave it here as a first reflection: a reminder that ideas from one domain often find unexpected echoes in another.
